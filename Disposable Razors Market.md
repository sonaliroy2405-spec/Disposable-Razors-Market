**Introduction**

As personal grooming continues to rise globally, the disposable razor market remains a dynamic segment within the broader razor and shaving industry. Demand for affordable, convenient, and portable shaving solutions has kept disposable razors relevant for both occasional users and budget-conscious consumers alike.

**Brand Leadership and Competitive Landscape**

Industry analysts find that a handful of major manufacturers dominate the disposable razor space, while a mix of digital-native challengers and regional players compete for market share. Gillette, owned by Procter & Gamble, holds a leading position, capturing a significant portion of global disposable razor sales. Following Gillette, established brands such as BIC have secured notable shares, with other multinational companies and private-label offerings filling out the remainder of the market.

Industry data suggests that Gillette commands roughly 35.4% of the global disposable razor market, followed by BIC at about 18.7%, underlining the continued importance of scale and established retail distribution networks in maintaining market leadership. Beyond the traditional giants, players such as Schick (part of Edgewell Personal Care), Dollar Shave Club, and Harry’s have carved out niches, particularly through subscription-based models, digital engagement strategies, and differentiated product positioning. These newer business models emphasize recurring orders and direct online relationships with consumers  trends that have injected fresh competition into an otherwise mature segment.

**Trends Shaping Competition**

Several trends are reshaping the competitive battlefield. Subscription and e-commerce strategies are becoming central to success, with brands like Dollar Shave Club and Harry’s leveraging digital channels to build direct relationships with customers, offering convenience and often better unit pricing. Sustainability and product innovation are also gaining traction as consumers seek more environmentally responsible choices, though challenges remain given plastics’ dominant role in disposable razor construction. Regional market expansion, particularly in Asia-Pacific and other emerging regions, continues to drive sales, with rising disposable incomes and greater retail penetration boosting consumption.
